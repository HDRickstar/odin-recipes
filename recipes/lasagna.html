Lasagna Recipe

Description:

Lasagna is a rich, layered pasta dish that brings together tender sheets of noodles, savory meat sauce, creamy cheese, and aromatic herbs into one comforting bake. Traditionally made with wide, flat pasta layered between a hearty tomato sauce—often simmered with ground beef or sausage—lasagna is known for its depth of flavor. The sauce is typically seasoned with garlic, onions, basil, and oregano, creating a warm, fragrant base. Between the layers, a mixture of ricotta cheese, mozzarella, and sometimes Parmesan adds a smooth, creamy contrast to the robust sauce.

When baked, the top layer of cheese melts into a golden, bubbling crust while the inside remains soft and flavorful. Each slice reveals distinct, colorful layers that hold together yet remain tender. The combination of tangy tomato sauce, rich cheese, and perfectly cooked pasta creates a balanced bite that is both hearty and satisfying. Often served hot and fresh from the oven, lasagna is a classic comfort food that brings a sense of warmth and tradition to the table.

Ingredients:

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ground beef or Italian sausage
  • Tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • Tomato paste
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Egg (for ricotta mixture)
  • Fresh or dried basil
  • Dried oregano
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Olive oil

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Boil the lasagna noodles in salted water according to package directions. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Sauté chopped onion and garlic until soft and fragrant.
  4. Add ground beef or sausage to the pan and cook until browned. Drain excess grease if needed.
  5. Stir in tomato sauce, tomato paste, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Let the sauce simmer for 15–20 minutes to develop flavor.
  6. In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese with an egg, a pinch of salt, and a little Parmesan cheese until smooth.
  7. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a baking dish.
  8. Layer noodles over the sauce, then spread ricotta mixture, sprinkle mozzarella, and add more meat sauce. Repeat the layers until ingredients are used, finishing with sauce and a generous layer of mozzarella and Parmesan on top.
  9. Layer noodles over the sauce, then spread ricotta mixture, sprinkle mozzarella, and add more meat sauce. Repeat the layers until ingredients are used, finishing with sauce and a generous layer of mozzarella and Parmesan on top.
  10. Let the lasagna rest for 10–15 minutes before slicing and serving.
